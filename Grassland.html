Grassland

Here are 5 points about Grasslands: 1. Grasslands are areas dominated by a continuous cover of grasses, found in environments with more rain than deserts but less than forests[1][3]. 2. They are home to diverse wildlife, such as kangaroos, emus, ostriches, deer, antelope, rabbits, and prairie dogs in different parts of the world[1][4]. 3. Grasslands are known by different names in various regions, such as savannas and veldts in Africa, pampas in South America, and steppes in Europe and Asia[1][4]. 4. The Serengeti in Africa is a vast grassland supporting a variety of animals, including zebras, gazelles, wildebeests, cheetahs, lions, and leopards[1][4]. 5. Grasslands are agriculturally valuable habitats, filling the ecological niche between forests and deserts[5].
